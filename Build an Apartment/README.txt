EXTRA NOTES:
There was a lot to cover in this module. Everything from shaders, to lighting, to animation, state -- you name it. I think it provided a great overview of all of those things. It took me about a week to get through the course material, and then a few days to digest and make sure I understood the settings and the light baking stuff (...still trying to wrap my brain around all the details!). 

I spent an evening looking at the rubric and using the provided assets to get a working scene that had most of the elements. I had to spend another night troubleshooting the globe and state machine to get that working as specified. 

Once I had a working scene that met the requirements, I decided on a conceptual theme to take it to the next level. I settled on Noir because there are conventions to the genre that play well with the themes of this module. Mostly around lighting. The Noir genre features things like colored spotlights that cast shadows on walls, so I figured that might be good for this. Also, by adding sounds and effects, I could help set a dark mood that helps transport the viewer into another world.
